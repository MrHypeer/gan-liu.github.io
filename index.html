Research

Quality Control in Additive Manufacturing and Its Application

Additive manufacturing (AM), especially selective laser manufacturing (SLM) has been employed to produce complex geometries housing internal channels, which is suitable to generate hydraulic components. However, the build quality like dimensional accuracy and surface roughness are still main obstacles that need to be addressed. This study utilizes a diamond-shaped channel and design of experiments methodology to investigate how the cross-section and process parameters influence the build quality. The results show that the diamond-shaped cross-section would promise less deformation in interior channels compared to the circular ones, but the surface roughness will not change too much even with varied parameters especially under specific build orientation.
With the compact circuit layout and small size, hydraulic components such as manifolds sometimes cause high pressure loss. This research investigates the pressure loss under different circumstances with various geometry features and present solutions to reduce pressure loss. The pressure loss performance is evaluated by both experimental and numerical methods. The optimal geometrical features are selected by means of full factorial experiments to balance the pressure loss, which also takes space requirement into consideration. Moreover, the novel design is built using additive manufacturing (also known as 3D printing), and results show that the pressure loss can be reduced considerably by adopting a smooth transition, where the reduction can reach up to 50%.
Hydraulic system, especially mobile hydraulic system like robots has a limited installation space and packed components. Heat generated from throttle loss has a profound impact on the whole system's performance, which may worsen internal leakage, degrade the oil quality and deteriorate the control performance. So, if a passive heat dissipation system is integrated, this will guarantee compact and integrated robots that do not need additional energy to transfer heat away. By means of additive manufacturing, conventionally impossible geometry like triply periodic minimal surfaces (TPMS) can be built. An ongoing investigation compares the performance enhancement with traditional heat sink and heat exchanger, and further research is needed when this novel geometry applied to robots.

Research on Energy Efficient Design in High-Speed Piston Pump

An optimization model is established for the multi-scale textured cylinder/valve plate interface in axial piston pumps by coupling the lubrication model of the oil film, the cylinder dynamic model and the multi-objective optimization model based on the Non-dominated Sorting Genetic Algorithm (NSGA-II). The time-varying cylinder micro-motions are considered. Aiming to minimize both the mechanical and volumetric losses, optimal dimple shapes are obtained for an industrial pump and an electro-hydrostatic actuator (EHA) pump. The millimeter-scale interfaces which are large-scale textured with hundreds of the optimal micron-scale dimples are evaluated. After comparing two dimple arrangements for the EHA pump, the athletic-track-shaped and water-drop shaped dimples are found to reduce the friction torque and leakage by 8.2% and 9.9% on average, respectively.
Churning loss has a significant influence on the mechanical efficiency in axial piston pumps. This research focuses on experimental investigations on the churning losses and introduces a test rig to measure the influence of various parts on churning loss in an axial piston pump. The description of the test method is presented in detail, followed by the sample results of the churning losses measurement. According to the measuring results under test conditions, it is found that the churning loss is mainly related to cylinder block and pistons, and has little relation with swash plate and variable mechanism.

A Hydraulic Buffer Used in Shipbuilding

A hydraulic buffer is designed to prevent a test body escaping from designed space. This design adopts the energy-stored components like accumulators and also the bulk property of hydraulic oil. Design concept is validated using numerical simulation and preliminary tests, and the proper specifications of hydraulic components are chosen carefully. On-site experiment shows that this device can function well and can tolerate a peak shock of 20tf.
